# VANTS
**Brand Identity Design Briefing** *Prepared by Wlad Mendes | March 2026* *Confidential — for design team only*

---

## 1. The Brand

### WHAT VANTS IS
Vants is a mobile financial app for Latin America. It gives people a single place to invest in high-yield products and pay real-world bills — electricity, internet, phone, rent — directly from those investments.

The key insight: instead of cashing out large sums in advance and guessing how much to keep and how much to spend, Vants lets you keep everything invested and earning.

When it’s time to pay a bill, the app sells exactly the right amount at the moment of payment and settles the bill instantly. Your money stays invested and earning until the second you need it.

### ONE LINE
> **Your investments. Your payments. One global account.**

### THE CORE LOOP
The entire product is built around three words:
**Invest → Earn → Pay**

Users deposit into yield-bearing products. Those products earn returns continuously. When a bill arrives, the app intelligently converts just enough to cover the payment and settles via local rails (SPEI in Mexico, Pix in Brazil, CBU in Argentina). The rest keeps compounding. No manual steps, no guessing, no wasted time.

### WHAT VANTS IS NOT
* **Not a crypto exchange.** Users don’t come here to trade.
* **Not a DeFi dashboard.** There are no protocol names, no wallet addresses, no gas fees visible.
* **Not a crypto wallet.** No seed phrases, no blockchain jargon.

Vants is a financial app. It competes with Nubank, Revolut, and Mercado Pago — not with Binance or MetaMask.

## 2. Brand Personality

### THE FEELING WE WANT
When someone opens Vants, they should feel the same confidence they feel opening their best banking app — but with the knowledge that their money is working harder for them. It should feel trustworthy, modern, and effortless. Not techy, not complex, not “crypto-bro.”

### PERSONALITY TRAITS

| Trait | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | We know our product is powerful but we don’t show off. Clean, understated elegance. |
| **Warm, not cold** | Financial apps can feel clinical. Vants should feel welcoming and human — like a trusted friend who’s great with money. |
| **Smart, not complicated** | The intelligence is in the system, not in the interface. The UI should be so simple it feels obvious. |
| **Latin American, not generic** | We’re building for Mexico, Brazil, Argentina. The design should resonate culturally — not be a Silicon Valley clone. |

### BRAND VOICE
If Vants were a person, it would be a financially savvy friend in their 30s who lives in São Paulo or Mexico City. They’re sharp but approachable. They explain things simply, never condescendingly. They celebrate your wins and make you feel in control of your financial life.

### WE SAY
* “Your money keeps earning until the moment you need it.”
* “Pay your electricity bill in 5 seconds. Your investments handle the rest.”
* “Stop choosing between saving and spending. Do both.”

### WE NEVER SAY
“Yield farming,” “smart contracts,” “gas fees,” “on-chain,” “DeFi,” “blockchain,” “wallet address,” “seed phrase,” “protocol.”

These words never appear in the product or marketing. The technology is invisible.

## 3. Strategic Context

### THE MARKET
* **391M+ people** across Mexico (130M), Brazil (215M), and Argentina (46M).
* These countries have instant payment rails (SPEI, Pix, CBU) used daily by hundreds of millions.
* High-yield digital investment is growing fast, but no app connects investments directly to everyday payments seamlessly.

### THE USER
Our primary user is a 25–40 year old urban professional in Latin America. They already use Nubank, Mercado Pago, or Revolut daily. They’re financially aware — they want better returns than banks offer — but they don’t want to become a finance expert. They want their money to grow AND be usable without friction.

### COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Our users currently choose between:
* **Traditional banks (Nubank, BBVA)** — great UX, terrible yields (< 2%), no connection between savings and spending.
* **Investment apps (GBM, Warren)** — better yields, but money is locked. To spend, you withdraw, wait, transfer. Friction kills the experience.
* **Crypto-native apps (Bitso, Lemon)** — highest yields, but feel like trading platforms. Too many steps to actually use the money.

### WHERE VANTS SITS
The usability of Nubank + the returns of an investment app + the instant settlement of SPEI/Pix. **All in one place.**

## 4. Visual Direction

### DESIGN STYLE
**Commercial. Financial. Modern. Premium.**

The visual identity should communicate financial seriousness and trust while being approachable and inviting. Think: a fintech that adults trust with their money, designed with the craft of a premium consumer brand.

### MOOD

| Mood | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Trust** | Stability, security, institutional confidence. This is where people put their money. |
| **Growth** | Upward movement, compounding returns, momentum. Your money is always working. |
| **Simplicity** | Clarity, zero clutter, instant understanding. If it needs an explanation, it’s wrong. |

### VISUAL REFERENCES
Study these products for cues on specific qualities:
* **Nubank (Brazil)** — Warmth, accessibility, bold color on minimal surfaces. How they own a single brand color.
* **Revolut Premium / Ultra** — Premium financial tool in your pocket. Elegant card design. Material quality.
* **Mercury (US)** — Clean typography hierarchy. Data density without clutter. Professional without boring.
* **Robinhood** — Simplified investing for regular people. Celebration moments. Emotional design.
* **Mercado Pago (LatAm)** — The QR payment flow Latin Americans already know. Speed and confirmation patterns.

### WHAT TO AVOID
* **No crypto clichés** — No blockchain cubes, neon glow, circuit patterns, coin illustrations, rocket ships.
* **No dashboard overload** — We’re not TradingView. Our user is paying a bill, not analyzing candle charts.
* **No gradient abuse** — One or two purposeful gradients max.
* **No generic fintech** — We should not look like “another Nubank clone.” We need our own distinct character.

## 5. Logo & Wordmark

### DIRECTION
The Vants logo should work in two formats:
* **Logomark (icon)** — App icon, favicon, avatar, loading screen. Recognizable at 32x32px. Abstract preferred.
* **Wordmark** — “VANTS” in the brand typeface. Slightly tracked letterforms for a premium feel.

### CONCEPTUAL TERRITORIES TO EXPLORE
1.  **The Gateway** — Passage, movement, transition. The bridge between investing and spending.
2.  **The Shield** — Protection, security, control. Your money, your account.
3.  **The Flow** — Continuous loop. Invest→Earn→Pay. Money that never stops working.

*The team is free to explore beyond these. Starting points, not constraints.*

### CONSTRAINTS
* Works on dark and light backgrounds. 
* Legible at 24px height. 
* Single-color versions in white and black.
* No text in the logomark.

---

## 6. Typography Direction

### WHAT WE NEED
A type system that feels financial and modern but not cold. Numbers are heroes — portfolio values, returns, payment amounts — so the typeface must look beautiful at large sizes. The team should propose a full typeface system.

### CONSIDERATIONS
* **Display** — Geometric or neo-grotesque sans-serif with personality. Designed, not defaulted to.
* **Body** — Highly legible at 14–16px on mobile.
* **Numbers** — Precise and trustworthy. Tabular figures essential. Portfolio values are hero content.

---

## 7. Motion & Emotion

### KEY MOMENTS
* **Payment confirmed** — The most important moment. Bill paid in under 5 seconds. Celebrate it. Our “Robinhood confetti” moment.
* **Returns growing** — Portfolio value should feel alive. Smooth number transitions as yields accumulate.
* **First deposit** — A milestone worth acknowledging. Welcome to Vants.

*The team defines the motion language: easing, durations, balance between celebration and restraint. Financial apps need confidence, not carnival.*
